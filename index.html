About

Chess is my favourite laboratory for studying reasoning. With collaborators in the Computational Social Science Lab I built ChessQA, a benchmark for chess understanding in language models, distilled master-level play into grounded chess reasoning, and developed ThinkTwice, which jointly optimizes models for reasoning and self-refinement.

Before that I spent two years in Scott Sanner’s Data-Driven Decision Making Lab working on retrieval and natural-language recommendation: elaborative query reformulation, Gaussian-process item scoring with LLM relevance judgments, and manifold-aware metrics for dense passage retrieval (EMNLP 2025). At Air Canada I put some of this into practice and released TravelDest, the first benchmark dataset for travel search. I also study where these systems break: SafeGEO examines how generative engine optimization can manipulate recommendation agents.

Earlier I worked on motion planning for autonomous driving at Bosch. Outside the lab I am co-founder and CTO of ZBot Technology, building an export-oriented AI agent system.
